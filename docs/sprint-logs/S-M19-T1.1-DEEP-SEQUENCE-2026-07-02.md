## Validation Performed
**Gate-check (purged-CV, 5 folds, ~87.6k eval bars):**

| Model | macro_f1 | f1_volatile | accuracy | volatile base rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TCN** `btc-regime-15m-tcn-v1-gpuburst` | **0.534** | **0.162** | 0.835 | 3.6% |
| LightGBM incumbent (experiment.json) | 0.582 | 0.190 | 0.950 | 2.0% |
| LightGBM incumbent (registry entry) | 0.560 | 0.390 | 0.630 | 14% |

**Verdict — CLEAN NEGATIVE.** The TCN is below the LightGBM head on BOTH macro_f1 and
f1_volatile against either incumbent metric set. The win bar (beat on macro_f1 AND
f1_volatile by >0.01) is met on neither → **no promotion; TCN stays at `candidate`
(inert).** This is exactly the design doc's predicted outcome: a deep model over raw
bars does not beat the class-weighted boosted tree on this label/data regime — the
value of depth is not here. Cost of the answer: ~$0.20 of GPU.

**Honesty caveat:** the base rates are not perfectly matched (TCN 3.6% vs the
incumbent's on-disk 2%/14%), so this is not a laboratory-clean A/B. But the TCN's
*absolute* macro_f1 (0.534) sits below the LightGBM's known ~0.55–0.58 range at this
operating point, so the negative is robust to the base-rate quibble. A perfectly
controlled re-run (train the LightGBM head on the identical `market_features` the TCN
used, same 0.005 threshold) is logged as a follow-up (MB below) — but is not expected
to overturn a ~0.05 macro_f1 gap.
