## Validation Performed
**Encoder (widened panel):** val_loss **1.29** (down from 2.01 on the thin panel;
recon 0.031, n_series 28, ONNX parity 1.7e-05) — breadth cut the encoder's
overfit ~35%, as diagnosed.

**Downstream purged-CV A/B (v006, identical folds, all 3 arms on one dataset):**

| arm | macro_f1 | f1_volatile | accuracy | f1_range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| base | 0.559 | 0.389 | 0.628 | 0.728 |
| emb (T0.1) | **0.611** | **0.423** | 0.705 | 0.799 |
| corpusemb (T1.2) | 0.584 | **0.269** | 0.825 | 0.898 |

Sanity check: base + emb barely moved v005→v006 (they don't read `corpus_emb`;
~0.002 fold noise), so only the corpus arm changed.

**Verdict — CLEAN NEGATIVE, replicated across two corpus widths.** `corpus_emb`
beats neither the baseline nor the T0.1 emb on macro_f1, and f1_volatile got
*worse* with more corpus width (0.303→0.269) while accuracy/f1_range climbed —
the embedding buys majority-"range" accuracy by crushing the minority "volatile"
recall the vol-gate keys on. Root cause: **representation-target mismatch** — a
daily macro/rates/FX/credit panel carries risk-regime backdrop, not the 15-min
volatility-burst signal; a better-generalizing encoder (val_loss 2.0→1.3)
produced a *worse* head. Third independent negative on price-representation.
