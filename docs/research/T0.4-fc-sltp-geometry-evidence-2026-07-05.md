# T0.4 Phase-2 — fc-informed SL/TP geometry: offline backtest evidence (2026-07-05)

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE — not a validated win. The offline simulator fails its
own reality-calibration check, so the apparent fc-vol-scaling edge cannot be
trusted as a real-money estimate. Do NOT graduate an fc→SL/TP-geometry change on
this evidence.**

## Question

M19's T0.4 `fc` forecast head is the milestone's one durable win as a **vol-regime
classifier feature** (see the T0.4 row in `ROADMAP.md`). Phase 2 asked a *different*
use of the same forecast: instead of feeding `fc_*` as a feature, **size the
stop/target from the forecast's own relative range** — would scaling the SL/TP
*distance* by the forecast's predicted volatility have improved historical trade
outcomes?

## Method

Tool: `scripts/ml/fc_sltp_geometry_backtest.py` (merged #5587). Tier-1, read-only —
reads the synced `trade_journal.db` closed trades + the `datasets-out/{forecasts,
market_raw}` side-streams, writes nothing, never touches the order path.

**v1 was a confounded strawman** (an "absolute 15-minute quantile" barrier vs the
actual multi-hour SL/TP — a tight coin-flip that is not apples-to-apples). It was
**not** reported as a result. v2 is the confound-removed **three-arm** design, each
arm internally consistent, holding each trade's **real direction and R:R fixed** so
the *only* variable is the stop/target **distance**:

| arm | what it measures |
|---|---|
| `real-realized` | `pnl / risk_$` — the trade's ground-truth realized R (no re-sim). **The anchor.** |
| `fixed-resim` | the actual SL/TP re-simulated forward via triple-barrier (does the engine track reality?) |
| `fc-vol-scaled` | actual R:R, stop/target distance × `clamp(fc_range_rel / median_fc_range, [0.5, 2.0])`, same engine |

Because scaling both SL and TP by the same ratio **preserves r_tp** (the R:R is
identical), `fc-vol-scaled` vs `fixed-resim` isolates one thing: how the absolute
barrier distance interacts with the forward walk and the `--max-hold` mark-to-close
cap. The `real-realized` anchor exists so nothing rests on a re-sim we haven't
sanity-checked against reality.

Ran on the trainer VM (relay #5588) against the full synced journal:
`--symbols BTCUSDT,ETHUSDT --max-hold 96` (96 bars = 24h at 15m). `skipped: {no_fc_cover: 0, bad_row: 0}` — full fc coverage, fc span 2021-07-01…2026-06-30.

## Results

BTCUSDT (the only symbol with real volume; ETH has n=20, too thin to weigh):

| arm | n | win_rate | mean_R | sum_R | maxDD_R |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| real-realized | 458 | 0.264 | **−0.675** | −309.0 | −346.9 |
| fixed-resim | 552 | 0.254 | **−0.058** | −31.8 | −101.5 |
| fc-vol-scaled | 552 | 0.390 | **+0.416** | +229.8 | −44.4 |

At face value `fc-vol-scaled` looks spectacular (+0.42R vs −0.06R). **It is not a
win**, for one decisive reason the anchor surfaces:

### The simulator fails its reality-calibration check

`real-realized` (**−0.675R**) and `fixed-resim` (**−0.058R**) diverge by ~0.6R.
These are supposed to be the *same* SL/TP placements — one as actually-realized pnl,
one re-simulated. A 0.6R gap means **the forward triple-barrier engine does not
reproduce reality.** Real BTC trades lose ~0.67× their intended risk on average; the
re-sim of those same placements says they'd be roughly flat.

The gap is expected and explains itself: the re-sim answers a **counterfactual the
live system does not run** — "hold to SL/TP or the 24h cap." Live trades instead
close for many other reasons (fees/slippage, monitor exits, flip-policy closes,
netting-guard/reconciler closes) — mostly *not* at a clean barrier. So `fixed-resim`
is a different (and rosier) exit policy than the live one.

### Therefore the fc-vol-scaled "edge" is an in-simulator artifact

`fc-vol-scaled` runs on the **same** engine as `fixed-resim`, so its +0.47R apparent
improvement is measured *inside a simulator that is already ~0.6R too optimistic vs a
losing ground truth*. The improvement comes entirely from how scaled barrier distance
interacts with the 24h mark-to-close cap (tighter-in-low-vol barriers resolve within
the window differently) — a real mechanical effect, but one that says nothing
validated about real-money P&L until the simulator reproduces live exit behavior.
"Improvement over a rosy broken baseline" is not a tradeable result.

## Conclusion & recommendation

- **The `real-realized` anchor did its job** — it is exactly why v2 exists, and it
  stopped a simulator artifact from being sold as a win (the same discipline that
  voided v1).
- **fc→SL/TP geometry is NOT validated offline.** A faithful test needs a simulator
  that reproduces live exit behavior (matching `real-realized`) — or, more directly,
  a **live observe-only shadow-soak** that logs the fc-scaled geometry alongside the
  placed SL/TP (the same shape as the existing `exit_ladder_soak`) and compares
  realized outcomes. That is a larger build, not a one-command backtest.
- **No order-path change is proposed.** fc's *validated* use — the vol-regime
  classifier feature, at shadow, soaking toward the Tier-3 money gate — is unchanged
  and remains the milestone's real win.
- Follow-up logged to the ml-review backlog (`MB-20260705-FC-SLTP-GEOMETRY`).
